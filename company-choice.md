Selected Company: 
HealthCore

Selection Reasoning:
I chose HealthCore because they are a big company making 28 million dollars, but their technology is really behind. They have 12 clinics in different countries that don't talk to each other, and they are still using paper diaries and spreadsheets. I think this is a great chance to show how a smart AI agent can fix these manual mistakes and help the clinics run more smoothly.

Challenge 1: Their biggest problem is that 22% of patients just don't show up. Since they still use phones and paper diaries to book people, they have no easy way to remind patients or fill those empty spots when someone cancels.

Challenge 2: They are losing a lot of money in the US because 14% of their insurance claims get denied. This happens because they don't have a single, clear way to see all their billing data in one place to catch errors.

Challenge chosen:
Challenge 1.

Role & Purpose:
The agent is basically a "Digital Assistant" for the front desk. Its job is to handle the boring back-and-forth texting with patients so the human staff doesn't have to. It makes sure every appointment slot is actually used.

What it does: 
It sends out reminder texts. If a patient says "I can't come," the agent automatically cancels them in the system and texts the next person on the waiting list to see if they want that spot.

User Interaction:
The clinic staff won't have to do much. They will just look at a screen that shows a "Clean Schedule" where the agent has already confirmed everyone. The staff only has to jump in if a patient has a really difficult question that the AI can't figure out.
